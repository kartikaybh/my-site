# Panel Discussion — Kartikay Bhardwaj Website Review

---

**Moderator:** All five of you have reviewed the site independently. Let's find the consensus and the conflicts.

---

## Round 1: What's actually working?

**Shreya:** The hero tagline is the best piece of writing on the site. "Nine years. Every level of government. Every kind of brief. And still the one who answers the call." That's specific, rhythmic, and human. It's the one line I'd remember.

**Marcus:** Visually the site is strong. The typography system is intentional — Cormorant Garamond for display, Jost for UI. The cream-and-gold palette works without being ostentatious. The scroll animations are smooth. It looks credible.

**Rohit:** The background is genuinely rare. XLRI + ISB + 9 years at Verse + Comscore credibility — this is not a generic consultant. The site conveys that, even if it doesn't lean into it hard enough.

**Ankit:** The services section is clear. Three services, each with a result line. That's the right structure. I just wish the result was at the top instead of the bottom.

**Meera:** The "Who I Work With" section immediately told me I was in the right place. Seeing "State Governments" and "PSUs" in the first row of that grid was reassuring.

---

## Round 2: Where does everyone agree it's broken?

**Marcus:** Mobile navigation. At 480px all nav links disappear. No hamburger, no alternative. That is a launch blocker. Full stop.

**Ankit:** Agreed. And while we're on mobile — there's no WhatsApp button anywhere on the site. He literally sells WhatsApp marketing as a service. The cognitive dissonance is real.

**Meera:** The testimonials. "PSU Client." "Government Client." Those aren't testimonials — they're placeholders with quotes. I noticed immediately. It actually lowered my trust.

**Rohit:** Same. Real testimonials or none at all. Placeholder testimonials in a sector where relationships are everything is a credibility own-goal.

**Shreya:** And the contact email is `hello@kartikay.com` — a domain that doesn't appear to be live. Is that intentional? Because if a government comms officer tries to verify the email domain and gets nothing, that's a problem.

---

## Round 3: The deeper disagreements

**Ankit:** My main concern is that this site is built for warm leads. If you already know Kartikay, the site does its job. If you're coming in cold — via search, via a referral you haven't pre-sold — there's not enough here to convert you. No case studies, no phone number, no downloadable deck.

**Rohit:** I'd push back slightly. For a personal brand at his level, warm leads might be 90% of the audience. The question is whether the site serves those warm leads well enough to be the thing that tips them over. Right now it doesn't — because the testimonials are fake, there's no social proof, and Greynium is barely mentioned.

**Shreya:** The copy problem is related. Several sentences sound like they were written by a copywriter who interviewed him for 20 minutes rather than by someone who knows him deeply. "Depth over flash, outcomes over optics, trust over tactics" is the worst offender. No one talks like that. It's alliterative filler.

**Marcus:** From a design standpoint, the CTA is duplicated — "Work with me" in the hero and "Start a conversation" in the banner, both linking to the same contact form. Pick one label and be consistent.

**Meera:** The thing that bothers me most as the actual target customer: there's no signal that he understands government procurement constraints. Can he work within GeM? Does he understand that payment takes 90 days? The site says "project briefs and monthly retainers" — that's agency language, not government language.

---

## Round 4: The one thing each person would fix first

**Shreya:** Replace the three "depth over flash" style triples with one genuinely specific observation about institutional India. Make it something only Kartikay could say.

**Marcus:** Add a mobile hamburger menu. Today. This is non-negotiable before any traffic lands on this site.

**Ankit:** Add a WhatsApp CTA button. It takes 10 minutes and removes the biggest conversion gap for his actual audience.

**Meera:** Add one real case study. Doesn't need to name the client. Even "a state government department's digital campaign — objectives, approach, result" would transform the credibility.

**Rohit:** Make Greynium.com a real entity on this site. Give it a sentence or two of context. Right now it appears once in the About section and disappears. If this is the business he's building, it deserves to be positioned, not footnoted.

---

## Consensus Summary

The site is **launch-ready with three conditions:**

1. Fix mobile navigation (launch blocker)
2. Remove or replace placeholder testimonials (credibility blocker)
3. Add a WhatsApp or phone contact option (conversion blocker)

Everything else is important but can follow post-launch.
