# Shreya Iyer — Brand Strategist Review

## First impression
The aesthetic is doing a lot of the heavy lifting. Cormorant Garamond, warm cream, gold rule — it signals "serious person." That part works. But when I start reading, the copy doesn't quite match the visual promise. It's good. It's just not *his*.

## What's distinctive
The hero tagline — "Nine years. Every level of government. Every kind of brief. And still the one who answers the call." — is the best line on the site. It's specific, it has rhythm, and it makes a quiet claim that lands. I would remember that line.

The CTA section — "You've worked with enough agencies. This is different." — also works. It's confident without being arrogant.

## What sounds like AI (or a template)
Several phrases read like polished-up generic copy:

- *"depth over flash, outcomes over optics, trust over tactics"* — this is a triple-rhythm construction that sounds written, not said. Kartikay would never say this out loud.
- *"Performance-led paid campaigns across digital platforms. Built to convert, not just to reach."* — every performance agency says exactly this.
- *"Strategy first, execution second."* — said by every agency on earth.
- *"India reads WhatsApp. I build campaigns that work with that behaviour — not against it."* — better, but still slightly rehearsed.
- The About section pivot from "I didn't plan to…" to "depth over flash, outcomes over optics, trust over tactics" loses the personal voice it had built.

## The big brand problem
There is no point of view on India. Kartikay works with state governments, PSUs, political campaigns — this is deeply specific institutional India. But the site could belong to anyone who does digital marketing anywhere. Where is the texture? The specificity? A line that only *he* could write?

What does institutional digital India actually look like in 2025? What's broken about how it's currently done? That tension — stated plainly — would make this site unforgettable.

## Would I remember this site?
Yes, mostly because of the design. Not because of the copy. The copy is good but generic in places where it needs to be razor-specific.

## Verdict
**7/10.** Strong visual brand identity. Copy is 70% there. Needs one or two moments of genuine specificity — a real observation, a real frustration, a line that couldn't have been written by anyone else.
