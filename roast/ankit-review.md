# Ankit Verma — Growth Marketer Review

## 5-second test
**What does this person do?**
Landing on the hero: I see "Kartikay Bhardwaj" massive on screen. Below it: "Digital Media Strategist · India." Then an italic tagline about nine years and government.

I understand: this is someone senior in digital media with a government background.
I do NOT immediately understand: what exactly he will *do for me* and what it will cost me to find out.

Pass — but barely. The eyebrow "Digital Media Strategist" is generic. "Digital advertising for governments and corporates" would be faster and clearer.

## Value prop clarity
The services section is the clearest part of the site. Digital Ads, Content/Influencer, WhatsApp — clean, understandable. But:
- Each service description talks about *process* ("strategy first, execution second") not *outcome* ("your campaign reaches 2M voters in 6 weeks").
- The result tags at the bottom of each card ("10x sales growth — delivered") are the best lines in the whole services section. They should be at the top, not buried at the bottom.

## Conversion flow
The conversion path is: Hero CTA → jumps to contact form at the bottom. That's a long scroll with no intermediate commitment step. No one who doesn't already trust you fills in a form after 30 seconds on a website.

**Missing:**
- No way to download something (a capabilities deck, a one-pager)
- No WhatsApp button (he literally offers WhatsApp marketing — why isn't there a WhatsApp CTA?)
- No LinkedIn link — for this audience, LinkedIn credibility is as important as the website itself
- No phone number — government and institutional clients pick up the phone

## Drop-off points
1. **After the hero** — users who aren't already sold will scroll once, see a wall of About text, and leave. The About section is too long and too narrative for first-time visitors who haven't bought in yet.
2. **Testimonials** — "PSU Client," "Political Campaign," "Government Client." No names, no specifics. Institutional clients reading this will notice immediately. Anonymous testimonials in this sector suggest the client relationships couldn't be named — which raises questions.
3. **Contact form** — asking for Name, Email, Organisation, and a Brief before any trust has been established is too much friction. A simple "Name + Email + What you need" is enough to start.

## What I'd fix for conversion
1. Add a WhatsApp button — primary CTA for this audience
2. Add LinkedIn link — secondary trust signal
3. Move result lines to the top of service cards
4. Shorten the About section or add a TL;DR
5. Reduce contact form to 3 fields
6. Add a phone number or at minimum a city

## Would I convert?
If I'm a government comms officer who found this via referral — yes. The site does enough to not embarrass itself.
If I'm a cold visitor who found this via search — no. Not enough immediate trust signals.

## Verdict
**6/10.** The site is built for warm leads, not cold traffic. That's a strategic choice — but it needs to be intentional, not accidental. Right now it falls short even for warm leads because there's no WhatsApp CTA, no LinkedIn, and no phone.
