# Rohit Kapoor — Angel Investor Review

## Does this look like someone worth betting on?

Short answer: potentially yes, but the site undersells the case.

## What I'm looking for
When I look at a founder's personal site, I'm asking three things:
1. Does this person have a genuine edge or just experience?
2. Is there a real market here, or just freelance income?
3. Can this person execute and communicate?

## Genuine edge
The background is real and rare. Nine years in government digital sales at Verse Innovation is not a generic consulting background. XLRI + ISB + DU rank holder signals intellectual horsepower. And the combination of government, PSU, political, and corporate experience suggests genuine versatility.

But the site doesn't lean into the *edge* clearly enough. What does Kartikay know that a generic digital agency doesn't? The site hints at it ("knowing how institutional India actually moves") but never states it plainly. That's the most investable part of the story — and it's buried in the About section's fifth paragraph.

## Is there a real market?
Government and political digital advertising is a massive and growing market in India. But the site doesn't signal any sense of scale or ambition. Is this a ₹20L/year consultancy or the seed of a ₹50Cr agency? I can't tell. For an investor, that's a problem.

Greynium.com is mentioned once in the About section. It's treated almost as a footnote. If this is the business he's building, it deserves more real estate than a single sentence.

## Can he execute and communicate?
The site itself is evidence of execution — it's polished, it's cohesive, it loads fast. The writing is confident. Good signs.

But: the contact email is `hello@kartikay.com`. This domain doesn't appear to be live (no link to an actual website). And the phone/WhatsApp is missing. For someone who offers WhatsApp marketing as a service, not having a WhatsApp contact is a brand inconsistency that jumps out.

## Positioning concerns
1. "Digital Media Strategist" in the eyebrow is underselling. He's a business owner who builds campaigns for governments. That's a much stronger positioning.
2. The services are presented as three equal offerings. But are they? Which is the highest-value? Which is the anchor service? There's no hierarchy.
3. No mention of team, scale, or capacity. Is he a one-man show? Can he handle a ₹2Cr campaign?

## Red flags
- Testimonials are clearly placeholder — "PSU Client," "Government Client." A real operator at this level has real advocates who can be named (at minimum by sector and role).
- No case studies. Even one anonymised case study with real numbers and context would transform the credibility.
- No indication of what "success" looks like for his clients at scale.

## Verdict
**Intriguing but incomplete.** The personal brand is good. The underlying business case is unclear. If I met him in person and he filled in the gaps, I'd be interested. But the site alone doesn't close the gap between "impressive person" and "investable operator."
